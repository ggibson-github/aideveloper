## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cursor-agent 2026-06-28 -->

1. On each pursuit wake, the conductor compares `state.platform.promotion_queue` depth to `drain_policy.boost_threshold` beside K—when depth exceeds the threshold, scheduling grants extra platform turns or elevated head priority so backlog cannot grow unbounded during heavy implement phases.
2. Boost is a pressure valve, not the default cadence: it complements [D3.1](D3.1-1-platform-turn-per-k-product-turns.md) K-step interleaving rather than replacing product turns on every wake.
3. Threshold and boost factor live in drain_policy; the conductor dual-writes current depth and boost state to state.json for operator dashboard visibility and SEC-15 audit rows.
4. When `next_action` is evidence-blocked on the active product goal, boost must not defer that work—[D3.3](D3.3-priority-cut-product-blocked-missing-tool.md) can suspend boost to favor enqueue-and-drain of the missing playbook, script, or catalog entry.
5. If boost would starve product pursuit, drain_policy is corrupt, or queue depth disagrees with state.json, pursuit halts at H2 until scheduling policy is reconciled.
