## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cli-composer-2.5 2026-06-28 -->

1. After each completed product phase during autopilot, the conductor or S0 scheduler reads `platform.drain_policy.product_steps_per_platform_turn` (K, commonly 5) and evaluates whether `steps_total % K == 0`.
2. When the modulo check is true and `platform.promotion_queue` is non-empty, the next pursuit iteration becomes a platform turn—[D2.3](D2.3-dequeue-platform-turn-not-product.md) dequeues head instead of running implement, design, or another product `next_action`.
3. When the promotion queue is empty at the K boundary, the scheduler skips the platform turn and product pursuit continues immediately—delivery does not idle waiting for self-improvement work.
4. Pack operators tune K without forking schedulers via [D5.1](D5.1-configure-task-level-params-only.md); baseline cadence still yields when [D3.2](D3.2-priority-boost-queue-depth-threshold.md), [D3.4](D3.4-idle-drain-product-waits-h2.md), or [D3.5](D3.5-max-platform-backlog-age-force-drain.md) overrides fire.
5. If `steps_total` drifts from journal dual-write or K is misconfigured to zero, scheduling fails closed at H2 until state.json and drain policy are reconciled.
