## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cursor-agent 2026-06-28 -->

1. Each promotion item in `state.platform.promotion_queue` carries age; after S0 preflight the conductor compares oldest backlog age to `drain_policy.max_platform_backlog_age` from the active pack ([D5.1](D5.1-configure-task-level-params-only.md)).
2. When oldest backlog exceeds the cap, force-drain policy schedules platform turns until age falls below the threshold or the queue empties—even if [D3.1](D3.1-1-platform-turn-per-k-product-turns.md) K-step cadence would defer the next platform drain.
3. Force drain is the adversarial-review backstop across D-branch scheduling: it prevents promotion debt from rotting during long product-only sprints without overriding active product evidence gates or clearing H2 blockers.
4. Partial promotion with re-enqueue resets item age; marking promotion done without passing [D6](D6-index.md) checklist fails closed and reopens the queue item at H2.
5. If force drain would starve evidence-blocked product work, the operator tunes age and force intensity per pack, or [D3.3](D3.3-priority-cut-product-blocked-missing-tool.md) suspends drain until the missing artifact class is enqueued.
