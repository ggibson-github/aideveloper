# A6.2: notify digest on H2 blocker not every step

**Parent:** [A6-index](A6-index.md) · **Branch A** · **Vision §3** · **Release:** v2.23

## Reader narrative
<!-- prose-source: agent plane-a 2026-06-28 -->

H2 blockers deserve prompt, consolidated notification; routine pursuit steps do not. This capability defines digest-style alerts on blocker detection—structured reason, goal id, suggested operator action—rather than per-step pings that train operators to ignore the channel.

The pursuit loop continues to dual-write blockers to journal/state; notification is an Plane I integration layer. Pair with session and daemon modes (A3) so unattended runs surface only actionable interruptions. Every-step notifications violate the minimal-HITL contract and recreate babysitting by another name.

## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cursor-agent 2026-06-28 -->

1. When pursuit dual-writes an H2 blocker to journal and state.json, Plane I integrations emit a digest notification with goal_id, structured stop reason, and suggested operator action—not a per-step ping.
2. Routine [A2.2](A2.2-if-ready-execute-one-pipeline-step.md) completions and counter bumps do not trigger notification channels; only actionable interruptions surface to operators.
3. Digest alerts pair with session and daemon modes ([A3](A3-index.md)) so unattended runs remain observable without alert fatigue that recreates babysitting.
4. Notification runs asynchronously after dual-write; pursuit does not block the loop waiting for webhook delivery per [A6.3](A6.3-operator-observe-without-unblocking-loop.md).
5. If notification dispatch fails, pursuit logs the gap in journal but continues unless operator policy requires delivery proof—observability failure becomes H2 only when configured as a hard gate.
