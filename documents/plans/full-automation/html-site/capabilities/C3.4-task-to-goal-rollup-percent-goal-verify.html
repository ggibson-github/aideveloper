C3.4 · Product execution plane

task, to, goal, rollup, percent, and goal verify

task to goal rollup percent goal_verify

Task completion rolls up to goal percent complete and goal_verify prerequisites: queue empty, all required evidence passed, conformance green. Rollup is S0 where scripts aggregate task state from state.json and journal mirror.

False rollup—marking goal ready while tasks lack evidence—is a Plane G failure class. Dashboard may show percent for operators; pursuit uses machine checks only.

How it works

  1. Step 1When platform promotion reaches L5, D4.6 pack-fragment-export distills stabilized L0–L4 artifacts from consumer repos into template-packs/ fragments—default pipelines, role bindings, verify suites, and catalog components new goals inherit at company instantiation.
  2. Step 2L5 fragments ship organizational capital upward from product promotions, not one-off edits in a single branch; pack authors validate against Plane F pack schema before publish and catalog INDEX marks discoverability.
  3. Step 3New goals at H1 pull active template-pack fragments through B5.1 role bindings and C1.4 pipeline registry—the conductor preflight loads inherited verify suites without re-deriving defaults in chat.
  4. Step 4L5 promotion closes only when D6.4 artifact-graph wiring notifies downstream goals of fragment changes and dual-write records promotion_queue completion to journal and state.json.
  5. Step 5If a fragment bypasses pack schema validation, forks without D5.3 provenance, or omits staleness graph wiring, platform pursuit fails closed at H2 until pack-fragment-export reconciles and restores invariant bindings.
