D3.2 · Platform evolution plane

priority, boost, queue, depth, and threshold

priority boost queue depth threshold

When promotion queue depth exceeds a configured threshold, priority boost accelerates platform drains—extra platform turns or elevated head priority—so backlog cannot grow unbounded during heavy implement phases. Boost is a pressure valve, not the default cadence; it complements D3.1 rather than replacing K-step interleaving.

Threshold and boost factor live in drain_policy beside K. Boost must not defer the current project goal when next_action is evidence-blocked; D3.3 can temporarily suspend boost to favor enqueue-and-drain of the missing artifact class. Track depth in state.json for operator dashboard visibility and SEC-15 audit rows.

How it works

  1. Step 1On each pursuit wake, the conductor compares state.platform.promotion_queue depth to drain_policy.boost_threshold beside K—when depth exceeds the threshold, scheduling grants extra platform turns or elevated head priority so backlog cannot grow unbounded during heavy implement phases.
  2. Step 2Boost is a pressure valve, not the default cadence: it complements D3.1 K-step interleaving rather than replacing product turns on every wake.
  3. Step 3Threshold and boost factor live in drain_policy; the conductor dual-writes current depth and boost state to state.json for operator dashboard visibility and SEC-15 audit rows.
  4. Step 4When next_action is evidence-blocked on the active product goal, boost must not defer that work—D3.3 can suspend boost to favor enqueue-and-drain of the missing playbook, script, or catalog entry.
  5. Step 5If boost would starve product pursuit, drain_policy is corrupt, or queue depth disagrees with state.json, pursuit halts at H2 until scheduling policy is reconciled.

Edge cases

  • Operator closes laptop mid-loop — state.json must resume from last good dual-write.
  • Concurrent manual edit to queue JSON — conductor reloads queue each wake; last writer wins with journal note.
  • Platform queue depth 0 but product blocked on missing playbook — D3.3 priority cut skips platform drain.
  • Edge case D3.2 variant 4: verify state dual-write before continuing pursuit.
