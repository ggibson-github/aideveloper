F1.2 · Organization plane

pack roles/*.yaml

pack roles/*.yaml

roles/*.yaml declares each organizational role: role_id, bound pipeline_id, tool permissions, MCP allowlists, KPI hints, and handoff targets. Roles are the unit of agent persona swap—workers inherit role context from spawn contracts while the conductor identity stays fixed (F6.1).

Mis-bound pipeline_id routes implement into wrong phases (B5.2). Per-role allowed_reads and evidence rules extend in F6.2 and F6.4. company.yaml lists role ids; this directory holds authoritative bindings.

How it works

  1. Step 1When program-scoper or the conductor loads a company pack, compose-first resolution (B4.3) queries the catalog (E1.7) for template-packs/_shared/ fragments before improvising role or verify content.
  2. Step 2Industry packs import _shared onboarding micro-packs, security baselines, and verify hooks through F5.1; the conductor binds merged artifacts to state.company.pack_id without duplicating shared YAML in each product repo.
  3. Step 3When pursuit promotes a mature fragment from a product repo, Plane D pack fragment export (D4.6) enqueues platform work; after L5 export lands in _shared, catalog regeneration must list the entry before company-autopilot resumes compose queries.
  4. Step 4Edits to _shared bump staleness for all importers—extend with backwards compatibility (D5.2) or fork a new catalog entry (D5.3) rather than silently breaking dependent packs.
  5. Step 5If an importer pack fails goal_verify after a _shared change without a compatibility path, pursuit stops at H2 with provenance notes until operators approve extend-or-fork at H1.
