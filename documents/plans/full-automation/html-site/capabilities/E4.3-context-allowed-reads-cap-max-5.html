E4.3 · Knowledge & composition plane

context, allowed reads, cap, max, and 5

context allowed_reads cap max 5

allowed_reads caps worker and Librarian context at five paths—hard bound on scope expansion. Librarian returns the cap; conductors pass it unchanged in spawn contracts; workers must not read outside the list except via declared tool output.

The cap forces compose and catalog search to prioritize (E2.1–E2.4) instead of loading entire design trees. Conductor may read more than workers but should still prefer INDEX and task cards. Violations are scope-bleed failure class; orchestrate-subagents enforces in worker prompts.

How it works

  1. Step 1After H1 plan approval, each pursuit wake begins with S0 preflight via A2.1 before the conductor routes any product or platform work.
  2. Step 2When preflight reports ready, the conductor executes exactly one pipeline step per turn—implement, design, or platform—then dual-writes journal and state.json before the next wake.
  3. Step 3Every K product turns, autopilot schedules a platform queue drain per D3.1 so Plane D promotion and catalog curation advance alongside the active goal.
  4. Step 4On scope completion, pursuit runs goal_verify, transitions to verifying, and requests H3 on pass per A2.5.
  5. Step 5If preflight finds blockers, budget limits, or goal_verify failure, pursuit stops at H2 with structured notes rather than advancing the queue or marking work complete silently.
