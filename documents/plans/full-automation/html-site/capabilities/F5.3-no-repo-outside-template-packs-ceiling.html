F5.3 · Organization plane

no repo outside template-packs ceiling

no repo outside template-packs ceiling

The template-packs ceiling rule: no organizational authority lives outside template-packs/ for pack-defined roles, pipelines, governance, and verify suites. Consumer repos instantiate packs; they do not silently fork company.yaml, roles, or pipelines into undocumented paths—this capability enforces auditability.

Urgent hotfixes flow through pack version bump, import, or operator policy override recorded in journal—not permanent shadow copies. Platform promotion targets pack fragments (D4.6); consumer-only patterns fail maturity claims. Vision §8 — Branch F — Organization plane (template-packs = ceiling) treats template-packs as whole-company organizational ceiling.

How it works

  1. Step 1state.hitl records pending as H1 plan approval, H2 blocker, H3 sign-off, or null when pursuit is unblocked, plus since and a payload with structured reason, goal_id, missing artifact, and suggested operator action.
  2. Step 2S0 preflight and check-pipeline-blocked read hitl.pending each wake; any non-null touchpoint halts autopilot until the conductor dual-writes clearance or the operator supplies an explicit approval or waiver phrase in the journal.
  3. Step 3Under J3 strict mode every H1/H2/H3 requires explicit operator clearance; default self-gate allows evidence-backed middle gates without treating Continue as approval (A5.2).
  4. Step 4Operators may observe pursuit status without clearing hitl.pending (A6.3); outbound notifications fire on H2/H3 only via I5.2, not on every pursuit step.
  5. Step 5If hitl.pending is H2 but the payload lacks required fields, the loop fail-closes and keeps goal.state blocked until dual-write restores a valid touchpoint record.
