Branch B — Cognition & routing plane

Plane B is cognition and routing: who acts on each turn (S0 scripts through S4 governance), at what model tier, and with what interleaved product-and-self-improvement schedule. The genius-tier conductor orchestrates; economy workers implement bounded task cards.

Deterministic-first is non-negotiable: if behavior exists under scripts/, S0 runs before improvisation. Plane B connects operator policy (model-policy.json) to everyday pursuit turns.

flowchart TD
  trigger[Trigger condition] --> pre[Preconditions S0]
  pre --> exec[Execute step logic]
  exec --> verify[Verify output]
  verify -->|pass| done[Mark queue item done]
  verify -->|fail| h2[H2 blocker]

What this defines

  1. Step 1On each pursuit wake, Plane B classifies the turn via B1 S0–S4: B1.1 scripts run before any LLM improvises when file-derived behavior exists under scripts/.
  2. Step 2The genius-tier B2.1 conductor merges worker summaries, reads route-tier.py output, and chooses next_action—spawning economy workers with bounded allowed_reads instead of implementing large task cards inline when spawn_workers is true.
  3. Step 3B3 model policy binds conductor to genius tier and workers to economy tier per docs/operator/model-policy.json, with B3.3 escalation when verification fails repeatedly.
  4. Step 4B4 routing interleaves product pursuit (next_action task each turn) with B4.2 platform queue peek/drain on the Plane D schedule so self-improvement advances without displacing the active goal.
  5. Step 5If a worker dual-writes journal/state, skips S0 preflight, or routes at the wrong tier, pursuit fails closed at H2 until the conductor reconciles routing policy—Plane B owns who acts, not what product step runs.
