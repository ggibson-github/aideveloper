C2.5 · Product execution plane

phase, inputs, outputs, verify, catalog, and refs

phase inputs outputs verify catalog refs

Each phase declares inputs, outputs, verify commands, and catalog refs so S0 and reviewers can audit completeness. Phase contracts live in skills and playbooks; pack schema may extend with pack-specific verify.

Missing catalog refs on phase outputs trigger compose-first divergence logging (B4.4). Phase verify is not goal_verify—it proves phase artifacts exist before next_action advances.

How it works

  1. Step 1When a company or program goal enters pursuit after H1, program-scoper loads company.yaml from the selected template-pack and dual-writes state.company.pack_id, default pipeline, and initial active_role into state.json.
  2. Step 2Before the first product turn, S0 preflight validates that company.yaml references only sibling pack artifacts—F1.2 roles, F1.3 pipelines, and F1.8 verify suites—rejecting ad hoc consumer-repo extensions at H2.
  3. Step 3During company autopilot (A3.3), the conductor reloads department and role topology from the bound pack so orchestrate-program lanes and handoffs align with pack structure without duplicating org charts in journal prose.
  4. Step 4Consumer goals pin pack version in pursuit state; the conductor imports pack metadata by reference rather than forking company.yaml inline, keeping template-packs the organizational ceiling.
  5. Step 5If schema drift, missing role references, or pack conformance failures surface during preflight or role switch, pursuit stops at H2 until pack metadata is reconciled and dual-written—never advance next_action on ambiguous org binding.
