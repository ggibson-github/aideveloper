D3.3 · Platform evolution plane

priority, cut, product, blocked, missing, and tool

priority cut product blocked missing tool

Priority cut inverts normal scheduling when product pursuit is blocked on a missing playbook, script, or catalog entry: platform drain targeting that artifact class jumps ahead of routine K-step interleaving. The product goal stays blocked on H2 or internal wait until the promoted artifact exists or operator waives.

Cut prevents absurd ordering—five implement turns while the very playbook needed for implement sits queued at priority 50. Cut does not clear human gates; it only reprioritizes platform work that unblocks product. Enqueue triggers (D2.1) may fire during the same blocked window; verify D6 checklist before marking promotion done.

How it works

  1. Step 1When product pursuit is blocked on a missing playbook, script, or catalog entry, preflight records the blocker and the scheduler applies priority cut—platform drain targeting that artifact class jumps ahead of routine D3.1 K-step interleaving.
  2. Step 2The product goal stays in blocked or internal-wait until the promoted artifact exists, D6 verify checklist passes, or an operator records an explicit H2 waiver—cut reprioritizes platform work but does not clear human gates.
  3. Step 3Enqueue triggers from D2.1 may fire during the same blocked window; the conductor deduplicates promotion items for the missing class and elevates head priority so implement turns do not run while the needed artifact sits deep in the queue.
  4. Step 4While cut is active, D3.2 depth boost is suspended so platform work that unblocks product precedes unrelated backlog-pressure drains.
  5. Step 5If cut misidentifies the missing artifact, promotion completes without unblocking product, or queue state disagrees with journal blockers, pursuit halts at H2 until compose-first catalog resolution reconciles the blocker.

Edge cases

  • Operator closes laptop mid-loop — state.json must resume from last good dual-write.
  • Concurrent manual edit to queue JSON — conductor reloads queue each wake; last writer wins with journal note.
  • Platform queue depth 0 but product blocked on missing playbook — D3.3 priority cut skips platform drain.
  • Edge case D3.3 variant 4: verify state dual-write before continuing pursuit.
