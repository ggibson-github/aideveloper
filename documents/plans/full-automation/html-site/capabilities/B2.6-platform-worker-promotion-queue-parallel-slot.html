Platform worker promotion queue parallel slot

Platform workers drain the promotion queue in parallel slots scheduled by Plane D policy—turning repeated one-off reasoning into scripts, playbooks, and pack fragments while product pursuit continues on the main stack.

The conductor assigns economy-tier platform workers with queue item ids; they must not mutate consumer goal scope. Completion updates platform maturity ladders and reconciles staleness for downstream catalog nodes. See B4.2 for peek/drain semantics each K product steps.

How it works

  1. Step 1On scheduled platform turns, the conductor assigns economy-tier platform workers a single promotion queue item id from Plane D without mutating the active consumer goal scope.
  2. Step 2Platform workers drain the assigned item—script extraction, playbook distillation, or pack fragment work—on a parallel slot while product pursuit continues on the main stack.
  3. Step 3Completion updates platform maturity ladders, catalog rows, and staleness nodes so downstream compose-first search sees the promoted artifact.
  4. Step 4Platform workers return bounded summaries; only the conductor dual-writes queue completion, journal notes, and state.json promotion fields.
  5. Step 5If a platform worker edits product goal artifacts, completes without verify proof, or blocks a product-blocked missing tool, pursuit halts at H2 and the queue item reopens rather than false-completing promotion.
