company_autopilot multi-goal role workstreams

Company autopilot extends single-goal pursuit to a multi-goal queue across roles and workstreams defined by an active template-pack (Plane F). The scheduler picks the next ready goal—respecting dependencies, platform slots, and role bindings—rather than assuming one state.json goal forever.

This mode is how “organizational reliability” scales: product, platform, and ops pursuits interleave without manual tab-switching, while H2 still pauses only the blocked stream. Conductor remains one writer; workers never dual-write journal/state. When one goal reaches H3 pending, others may continue if preflight allows. See SEC-17.6 decision on multi-goal stacks for deployment choices.

How it works

  1. Step 1With pursuit mode company_autopilot, the conductor maintains a multi-goal queue in state.json scoped by the active template-pack from Plane F—selecting the next ready goal by dependencies, role bindings, and platform slots rather than a single perpetual goal.
  2. Step 2Each wake runs A2.1 preflight against the active goal slice; H2 on one stream dual-writes a blocker for that goal only while other ready goals may still advance if preflight allows.
  3. Step 3B5.2 active_role rotates so the conductor context-switches pipeline_id, skills, and allowed_reads per selected goal—workers never dual-write journal or state.
  4. Step 4When one goal reaches H3 pending after A2.5, sibling goals in the company queue may continue pursuit if their streams are unblocked—organizational throughput without abandoning strict HITL on verified closures.
  5. Step 5If the scheduler picks a goal with unresolved dependencies or corrupt queue state, preflight fails closed at H2 until journal-keeper reconciles the multi-goal manifest.

Edge cases

  • Operator closes laptop mid-loop — state.json must resume from last good dual-write.
  • Concurrent manual edit to queue JSON — conductor reloads queue each wake; last writer wins with journal note.
  • goal_verify passes but H3 rejected — goal.state returns to pursuing with rejection notes.
  • Edge case A3.3 variant 4: verify state dual-write before continuing pursuit.
