context always-on rules AGENTS.md

Always-on context layers inject project rules and AGENTS.md every session—the baseline conductor contract, evidence rules, approval gates, and pipeline semantics. Workers inherit applicable rules via spawn context; they do not re-read the full AGENTS monolith unless allowed.

This is layer zero of context retrieval—below hooks (E4.2) and Librarian allowed_reads (E4.3). Pack authors may extend always-on rules through template-pack fragments. Changes to AGENTS.md mark dependent playbooks and skills stale in E5.2.

How it works

  1. Step 1At company instantiation, program-scoper binds state.company.pack_id and resolves roles, pipelines, governance, and verify suites only from declared paths under template-packs/—consumer repos instantiate packs rather than silently forking company.yaml or role YAML into undocumented repo locations.
  2. Step 2On every wake, compose-first routing (B4.3) and import resolution (F5.1) reject organizational authority referenced outside the template-packs namespace before the conductor assigns workers or advances next_action.
  3. Step 3When operators need an urgent organizational change, pursuit records a pack version bump, cross-pack import, or explicit policy override in the journal—not a permanent shadow copy that bypasses catalog and SEC-14 auditability.
  4. Step 4Mature patterns promoted from product pursuit must land as pack fragments via Plane D export (D4.6); consumer-only role or pipeline edits cannot satisfy maturity claims or goal_verify suites tied to the pack ceiling.
  5. Step 5If S0 or validate-workflow detects pack-defined authority living outside template-packs/, pursuit stops at H2 with the offending path recorded in journal and state.json until operators restore ceiling compliance or approve a pack update at H1.
