Decision self-gate rigor checklist vs reviewer

Open decision: how rigorous should middle design gates be when strict_hitl is off? Options span checklist-only self-gate, automated LLM reviewer, or second economy reviewer. The choice affects cost, latency, and mistake rates for HLD/DD/manifest phases.

Record the decision as an ADR when made; until then, implementers should default to checklist + evidence paths that goal_verify can audit.

What this defines

  1. Step 1When B5.1 sets active_role, S0 resolves role bindings from pack roles.yaml—pipeline_id, enabled skills, MCP servers, and forbidden paths—and merges operator overrides from model-policy.json into pursuit state.
  2. Step 2Before A2.2, route-tier reads pipeline_id from the active role so implement work cannot route into design-only phases; a platform role may invoke promotion-queue skills while a product role cannot waive governance gates.
  3. Step 3Worker spawn contracts under B2.3 inherit only the active role's skill allowlist and MCP/CLI permissions.—forbidden paths block reads even when workers are economy tier.
  4. Step 4At pack instantiation (F2.1), conformance scripts validate that each role's pipeline_id exists in the C1 registry and that skill bindings stay within tool permissions before the pack publishes.
  5. Step 5If role bindings drift after manual policy edits or reference an unknown pipeline_id, A2.1 halts at H2 until operators reconcile pack schema and state.json—never advancing implement with ambiguous routing.
