L1 playbook docs/playbooks

L1 promotes distilled procedure into docs/playbooks/<slug>.md: step lists, constraints, and pointers that humans and agents can follow without re-reading full design monoliths. Playbook-keeper skill (D4.1) is the primary platform worker for L1 creation from divergence logs and task post-mortems.

An L1 playbook is not a script—it encodes intent and ordering while leaving file-derived steps to future L2 extraction. INDEX rows and task-card references (D6.1, D6.3) prove the playbook is discoverable. Stale upstream design should bump dependents via Plane E staleness, not silent drift.

How it works

  1. Step 1Every conductor and worker session loads always-on context—E4.1 rules and AGENTS.md—as layer zero before hooks (E4.2) or Librarian allowed_reads (E4.3) narrow the turn.
  2. Step 2AGENTS.md encodes the baseline pursuit contract: evidence gates, H1/H2/H3 approval semantics, journal dual-write, and pipeline phase order that the genius conductor enforces each wake.
  3. Step 3Economy workers inherit applicable rule excerpts in spawn context; they do not re-read the full AGENTS.md monolith unless the conductor bound it in allowed_reads.
  4. Step 4Active template packs may extend always-on rules via pack fragments merged at role switch so company-specific policy layers atop repo defaults without replacing core conductor obligations.
  5. Step 5When AGENTS.md or always-on rules change, E5.2 marks dependent playbooks and skills stale and pursuit pauses at H2 until reconcile-stale or operator H1 confirms downstream artifacts are current.
