artifact-graph per program and pack

Artifact graph nodes represent design, code, test, and pack artifacts with dependency edges for program and pack scopes. reconcile-artifact-graph marks stale when upstream changes; pursuit respects stale flags before spawn.

Graph complements staleness.json for platform nodes; together they prevent implement against obsolete design. Pack schema may embed graph templates per company type.

How it works

  1. Step 1When S3 architecture ambiguity resolves during pursuit, the conductor dual-writes the session decision to journal Resolved Q&A and persists durable rationale as an ADR under docs/decisions/.
  2. Step 2ADRs record architecture choices, gate waivers with rationale, and program manifest conflicts—repo-authored judgment distinct from external truth in E3.1 facts files.
  3. Step 3Design artifacts and task cards link to settled ADRs when implement work depends on a recorded choice so workers load authoritative rationale via allowed_reads without re-litigating the trade-off in chat.
  4. Step 4H1-approved plans with open S3 trade-offs cannot spawn implement workers until the conductor files an ADR or documents the waiver in journal with explicit operator visibility.
  5. Step 5When an ADR supersedes prior assumptions, E5.1 staleness marks downstream design nodes and pursuit pauses at H2 until reconcile-stale or operator confirmation updates the artifact graph.
