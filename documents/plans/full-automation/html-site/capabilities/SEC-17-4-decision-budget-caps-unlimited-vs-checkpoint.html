Decision budget caps unlimited vs checkpoint

Open decision: budget caps for goal_autopilot—unlimited pursuit versus daily token/step checkpoints. Unlimited pursuit maximizes autonomy; checkpoints protect cost and surface H2 when budgets exhaust.

Budget fields in Plane A interact directly with this decision. Operators need dashboard visibility before choosing unlimited pursuit in production.

  verify -->|fail| h2[H2 blocker]

What this defines

  1. Step 1When A3.2 goal_autopilot is active, A1.4 budget fields in state.goal govern max steps, tokens, and wall-clock—operators choose unlimited pursuit versus checkpoint caps via this open SEC-17 decision.
  2. Step 2Each pursuit wake increments budget counters in state.json after A2.3 dual-write so dashboards and autopilot daemons share the same consumption picture before the next A2.1 preflight.
  3. Step 3Unlimited mode lets goal_autopilot run until goal_verify, a hard A4 block, or HITL—without arbitrary daily ceilings beyond operator-approved H1 plan bounds.
  4. Step 4Checkpoint mode stops pursuit when any budget exhausts and surfaces H2 via A4.3 resource stop taxonomy until the operator resets caps or approves continuation.
  5. Step 5If budget telemetry is missing or counters desync from journal step counts, pursuit fails closed at H2—never silently continuing goal_autopilot without visibility into cost burn.
