Branch A — Pursuit & control plane

Plane A is pursuit and control: the always-on loop that runs from approved intent until verification succeeds or a defined stop reason fires. It owns the goal model, preflight, one-step execution, post-step routing, goal-level verification handoff, and the semantics of Continue redefined for autonomy.

Every other plane feeds or constrains this loop—B routes execution, C performs product steps, G proves outcomes—but A is where "never stop until verified" lives. When reading A1–A6 capabilities, keep this chapter summary in mind as the architectural north star for that branch.

flowchart TD
  trigger[Trigger condition] --> pre[Preconditions S0]
  pre --> exec[Execute step logic]
  exec --> verify[Verify output]
  verify -->|pass| done[Mark queue item done]
  verify -->|fail| h2[H2 blocker]

What this defines

  1. Step 1After H1 approves intent, Plane A owns the always-on pursuit loop from A2.1 preflight through A2.2 execution until A2.4 goal_verify or an A4 stop reason fires.
  2. Step 2A1 supplies goal identity, machine-checkable success criteria, and budgets that every wake reads from state.json before routing—not cosmetic metadata for publication.
  3. Step 3A3 autopilot modes lift the per-session ceiling so goal_autopilot continues across daemon polls and IDE sessions until hard block or achievement without arbitrary chat limits.
  4. Step 4Plane B routes cognition, Plane C executes product phases, and Plane G proves outcomes—but A is where never stop until verified lives; other planes feed or constrain this loop.
  5. Step 5If pursuit violates Plane A contracts—skip preflight, advance on BLOCKED, or omit stop taxonomy—A4.4 integrity or verify stops halt at H2 fail-closed.
