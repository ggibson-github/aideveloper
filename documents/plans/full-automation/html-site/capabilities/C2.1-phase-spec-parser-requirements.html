phase spec-parser requirements

Spec-parser phase extracts requirements, validates assumptions, lists open questions, and creates or refreshes journal/state via journal-keeper. Blocking questions stop downstream design until answered or deferred with rationale recorded.

Outputs feed hld-writer and program-scoper when mega-spec detected. S0 scripts validate spec structure where templates exist. This phase is the H1 input artifact generator—pursuit should not skip to implement without parsed requirements unless journal says spec phase complete.

How it works

  1. Step 1When D3.1 schedules a platform turn after K product steps, the conductor dequeues the head of platform.promotion_queue without mutating consumer next_action or the active implement task.
  2. Step 2The conductor spawns economy platform workers—playbook-keeper, script extraction, or catalog regeneration—scoped to the item's target_level on the D1 ladder while product pursuit state stays frozen for that turn.
  3. Step 3One promotion item drains per platform turn unless parallel platform slots are explicitly enabled in policy, preserving an auditable queue mutation trail per dequeue.
  4. Step 4After workers finish, the conductor runs D6 definition-of-done checks—catalog row, L2+ verify script when applicable, task reference, staleness node—then dual-writes the item done or re-enqueues with updated partial state.
  5. Step 5If D2.2 schema validation fails, D3.3 blocks product on missing tooling, or D6 proof is absent, the item remains in-queue and pursuit fails closed at H2 rather than falsely completing promotion.
