orchestrate-program

Orchestrate-program skill spawns ready lanes when manifest approved, blocking questions cleared, and spawn_workers true. Each lane worker receives allowed_reads from Librarian briefing and manifest slice only.

Parent conductor merges lane summaries, updates artifact graph, and never clears gates without approval. Failed lane blocks program goal_verify until manifest reconciliation or H2 waiver.

How it works

  1. Step 1Before spawning workers, the conductor runs Librarian (readonly) and receives at most five allowed_reads paths derived from state.json, the active task card, and scoped playbooks.
  2. Step 2Spawn contracts pass allowed_reads unchanged to economy workers; workers must not open paths outside the list except through declared tool output captured as evidence.
  3. Step 3When context is insufficient, compose-first routing (E2.1–E2.4) ranks catalog components and playbooks instead of expanding the read set beyond the cap.
  4. Step 4The genius-tier conductor may read INDEX files and task cards beyond worker bounds but still prefers bounded briefing over loading entire design trees during orchestration turns.
  5. Step 5Scope-bleed attempts—workers reading forbidden paths or conductors omitting allowed_reads from spawn contracts—fail closed as G5.2 violations and stop at H2 until spawn contracts are corrected and dual-written.
