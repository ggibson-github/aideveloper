C5.3 · Product execution plane

definition, of, done, goal, slice, and satisfied

definition of done goal slice satisfied

Definition of done for a goal slice: tasks complete, tests pass (or documented exceptions), evidence on disk, goal_verify ready, no unresolved blocking questions. Differs from task done and phase done—slice satisfied means rollup checks pass for that scope boundary.

H3 still required for verified goal acceptance unless policy waives. Completion status in journal and STATUS.md reflects slice and goal levels distinctly so operators see partial vs achieved accurately.

How it works

  1. Step 1After S3 architecture merge or S4 H2 escalation resolves, the genius-tier conductor runs a short post-mortem before the next product turn—cataloging what repeated, what was improvised, and which playbook, script, or skill was missing at escalation root cause.
  2. Step 2Concrete improvement candidates from the post-mortem become conductor-only platform.promotion_queue items with source: post-mortem, suggested target_level, and priority override when the active goal is blocked on the missing artifact—workers never enqueue from post-mortem.
  3. Step 3Items may target L1 playbook first even when L2 script is the eventual goal, capturing institutional learning from failure rather than happy-path optimization on the D1 promotion ladder.
  4. Step 4Scheduling still follows platform turns per K product steps (D3.1); D3.3 can raise priority when product pursuit waits on the missing catalog entry without skipping open H2 resolution or human sign-off.
  5. Step 5If post-mortem finds no repeatable gap or H2 remains unresolved, enqueue is deferred and pursuit fails closed—promotion items are not created from speculation while the escalation is still active.
