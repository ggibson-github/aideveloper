active role, from, template, and pack

active_role from template pack

Active role comes from the instantiated template-pack: developer, operator, platform, legal reviewer—each with default pipeline_id and tool permissions. state.json carries active_role so pursuit, spawn policy, and HITL strictness match pack intent.

Switching roles mid-goal requires manifest or conductor decision recorded in journal; workers inherit role context in spawn contracts. Company autopilot (A3.3) schedules across roles without merging their permissions.

How it works

  1. Step 1On pack instantiation (F2.1), S0 loads roles.yaml from the active template-pack and sets state.active_role to the pack default for the current workstream—each role encodes pipeline_id, enabled skills, and tool permissions.
  2. Step 2Each pursuit wake reads active_role before A2.1 preflight so route-tier, spawn policy, and HITL strictness match pack intent—not the conductor's improvised defaults.
  3. Step 3When switching roles mid-goal, the conductor records manifest approval or an explicit journal decision, updates active_role in the same dual-write turn, and re-runs preflight so workers receive refreshed spawn contracts with role-scoped allowed_reads.
  4. Step 4Under A3.3 company autopilot, scheduling rotates across roles via F2.3 without merging permissions—each spawned worker inherits only its role's pipeline_id and tool allowlist.
  5. Step 5If active_role is missing, references an undefined pack role, or conflicts with manifest lane bindings, pursuit stops at H2 until operators repair pack config or record a valid role switch—never routing implement phases with ambiguous role context.
