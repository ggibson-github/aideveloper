H1.2 · Persistence & state plane

state, goal, and block

state goal block

The goal block records goal id, parent goal, goal type, success criteria, verify command, budget counters, and state enum (pursuing, blocked, verifying, achieved, rejected). Goal-verify is the terminal gate—pursuit loops until goal_verify passes or H3 rejects per A2.4.

Machine-checkable success criteria bind to evidence paths in Plane G; subjective criteria remain H3. Company autopilot schedules multiple goal blocks without merging their scopes (A3.3). Only the conductor writes goal transitions after verify evidence.

How it works

  1. Step 1At H1 plan approval, the conductor stamps goal id, parent_goal, goal_type, machine-checkable success_criteria, and verify_command into state.json goal block—establishing pursuit scope every wake reloads before routing.
  2. Step 2During pursuit, only the conductor transitions goal.state among pursuing, blocked, verifying, achieved, and rejected after verify evidence.
  3. Step 3goal_verify aggregates evidence from Plane G; subjective success language without machine checks remains H3, not autonomous achieved.
  4. Step 4Budget counters in the goal block increment each wake.; exhaustion dual-writes structured stop reasons without marking achieved.
  5. Step 5Company autopilot may schedule multiple goal blocks in parallel., but scopes never merge—evidence attaches only to the goal_id active on the current turn.
