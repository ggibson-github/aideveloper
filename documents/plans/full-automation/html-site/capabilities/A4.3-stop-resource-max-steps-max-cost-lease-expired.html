A4.3 · Pursuit & control plane

stop, resource, max steps, max cost, and lease expired

stop resource max_steps max_cost lease_expired

Resource stops bound autonomous pursuit in measurable units: max steps per session or goal, token budgets, cost caps, and lease expiry on shared runners. When a budget is exhausted, pursuit halts with a stop reason that operators can extend at H2 if policy allows.

These limits implement A1.4 in the stop taxonomy so “always-on” never means “unbounded.” Scheduling in Plane D may reprioritize work when leases expire, but the stop record must remain auditable. Too-tight budgets cause false stops; too-loose budgets waste spend—pack authors set defaults per goal_type.

How it works

  1. Step 1Each pursuit wake increments budget counters from A1.4—session steps, goal steps, token spend, cost accrual, and runner lease timestamps—before A2.2 schedules another phase.
  2. Step 2When any configured ceiling is reached, A2.1 emits stop class resource:max_steps, resource:max_cost, or resource:lease_expired and exits A2.6 without advancing next_action.
  3. Step 3Journal-keeper dual-writes the exhausted budget dimension, current counter values, and stop reason to journal and state.json so operators and Plane D schedulers can audit why pursuit halted—not a silent session end.
  4. Step 4Operators may extend budgets at H2 when policy allows; D3.2 may reprioritize sibling work when a shared runner lease expires, but the stop record remains until explicitly cleared.
  5. Step 5If budget fields are missing or counters disagree with ledger evidence, preflight fails closed at H2 rather than allowing unbounded always-on pursuit.

Edge cases

  • Operator closes laptop mid-loop — state.json must resume from last good dual-write.
  • Concurrent manual edit to queue JSON — conductor reloads queue each wake; last writer wins with journal note.
  • goal_verify passes but H3 rejected — goal.state returns to pursuing with rejection notes.
  • Edge case A4.3 variant 4: verify state dual-write before continuing pursuit.
