C2.2 · Product execution plane

phase, hld, dd, and diagrams

phase hld dd diagrams

Design phase produces HLD, DD (split api/data/ops), and diagrams from approved requirements. Default path uses self-gate plus evidence; strict HITL retains human HLD/DD approval gates from v2.13.

Diagram-generator runs after DD; staleness marks downstream task cards when design changes. Design artifacts live under docs/design/ and docs/diagrams/ per AGENTS.md paths. Conductor spawns dd-writer and diagram workers under S2 templates.

How it works

  1. Step 1When the platform queue dequeues an L1 promotion item, D4.1 playbook-keeper distills divergence logs and task post-mortems into docs/playbooks/<slug>.md—step lists, constraints, and pointers—without duplicating full design monoliths.
  2. Step 2L1 playbooks encode procedure intent and ordering only; file-derived mechanics remain for future L2 script extraction—the conductor does not treat L1 prose as executable S0 commands during product turns.
  3. Step 3On completion, playbook-keeper registers D6.1 INDEX rows and D6.3 task-card references so librarian briefings and implement turns can discover the playbook via allowed_reads.
  4. Step 4If upstream design or vision sections change, Plane E staleness marks dependent playbooks stale; platform turns reconcile or re-run playbook-keeper before product lanes cite the playbook in pursuit routing.
  5. Step 5If an L1 playbook lacks INDEX discoverability, was authored against stale upstream design, or duplicates an existing catalog entry without fingerprint dedupe, pursuit fails closed at H2 until playbook-keeper reconciles dependencies and dual-writes promotion-done state.
