notify digest on H2 blocker not every step

notify digest on H2 blocker not every step

H2 blockers deserve prompt, consolidated notification; routine pursuit steps do not. This capability defines digest-style alerts on blocker detection—structured reason, goal id, suggested operator action—rather than per-step pings that train operators to ignore the channel.

The pursuit loop continues to dual-write blockers to journal/state; notification is an Plane I integration layer. Pair with session and daemon modes (A3) so unattended runs surface only actionable interruptions. Every-step notifications violate the minimal-HITL contract and recreate babysitting by another name.

How it works

  1. Step 1When pursuit dual-writes an H2 blocker to journal and state.json, Plane I integrations emit a digest notification with goal_id, structured stop reason, and suggested operator action—not a per-step ping.
  2. Step 2Routine A2.2 completions and counter bumps do not trigger notification channels; only actionable interruptions surface to operators.
  3. Step 3Digest alerts pair with session and daemon modes (A3) so unattended runs remain observable without alert fatigue that recreates babysitting.
  4. Step 4Notification runs asynchronously after dual-write; pursuit does not block the loop waiting for webhook delivery.
  5. Step 5If notification dispatch fails, pursuit logs the gap in journal but continues unless operator policy requires delivery proof—observability failure becomes H2 only when configured as a hard gate.

Edge cases

  • Operator closes laptop mid-loop — state.json must resume from last good dual-write.
  • Concurrent manual edit to queue JSON — conductor reloads queue each wake; last writer wins with journal note.
  • goal_verify passes but H3 rejected — goal.state returns to pursuing with rejection notes.
  • Edge case A6.2 variant 4: verify state dual-write before continuing pursuit.
