runtime notify webhook email H2 H3 only

Optional webhook and email notifications fire on H2 blocker detection and H3 sign-off events only—not every pursuit step (A6.2). Payloads carry structured blocker reason, goal id, and suggested operator action.

Notification failure must not block pursuit; missed alerts are recoverable from journal Blockers section. Strict environments may disable outbound notify entirely; observation remains via dashboard (I5.1).

How it works

  1. Step 1When journal-keeper records an H2 blocker or requests H3 sign-off, the runtime may emit an optional webhook or email.—routine pursuit steps and phase completions do not trigger outbound notify.
  2. Step 2Notify payloads carry goal_id, structured blocker reason, and suggested operator action so recipients can act without opening the full journal.
  3. Step 3Delivery runs outside the critical pursuit path; webhook or SMTP failure logs locally and pursuit continues without waiting for operator acknowledgment.
  4. Step 4Strict environments may disable outbound notify in state or pack config; operators still observe status via I5.1 dashboard regeneration.
  5. Step 5If an alert was missed, the journal Blockers section and H3 pending state remain authoritative—notify is observability only and never substitutes for A6.3 clearance to resume autopilot.
