D3.5 · Platform evolution plane

max, platform, backlog, age, force, and drain

max platform backlog age force drain

Each promotion item carries age; when oldest backlog exceeds max_platform_backlog_age, force drain policy schedules platform turns until age falls or queue empties—even if K-step cadence would defer drain. This prevents the improvement backlog from rotting during long project-only sprints.

Force drain is the adversarial-review backstop cited across D-branch specs. It does not override active product evidence gates; it prevents promotion debt from rotting. Age resets on partial promotion with re-enqueue; false done still fails D6 audit. Operator may tune age and force intensity per pack.

How it works

  1. Step 1Each promotion item in state.platform.promotion_queue carries age; after S0 preflight the conductor compares oldest backlog age to drain_policy.max_platform_backlog_age from the active pack (D5.1).
  2. Step 2When oldest backlog exceeds the cap, force-drain policy schedules platform turns until age falls below the threshold or the queue empties—even if D3.1 K-step cadence would defer the next platform drain.
  3. Step 3Force drain is the adversarial-review backstop across D-branch scheduling: it prevents promotion debt from rotting during long product-only sprints without overriding active product evidence gates or clearing H2 blockers.
  4. Step 4Partial promotion with re-enqueue resets item age; marking promotion done without passing D6 checklist fails closed and reopens the queue item at H2.
  5. Step 5If force drain would starve evidence-blocked product work, the operator tunes age and force intensity per pack, or D3.3 suspends drain until the missing artifact class is enqueued.
