runtime notify STATUS dashboard generation

STATUS.md and docs/operator/dashboard.md regenerate from state.json and staleness manifest via generate-dashboard skill—S0 where script exists. Dashboards inform operators; they do not unblock pursuit (A6.3).

Session end and phase completion trigger regeneration in journal-keeper. Dashboard rows link to goal id, next_action, blockers, and evidence status for at-a-glance resume without opening full journal.

How it works

  1. Step 1After journal-keeper dual-write at session end or phase completion, the conductor runs generate-dashboard (B1.1 script or skill) to regenerate STATUS.md and docs/operator/dashboard.md from state.json and the staleness manifest.
  2. Step 2Dashboard rows surface goal_id, next_action, blockers, and evidence status so operators can resume pursuit without opening the full journal.
  3. Step 3Dashboard output is observational only.—regeneration does not advance next_action, clear H2, or substitute for H3 sign-off.
  4. Step 4IDE, SDK, and headless runtimes share the same S0 regeneration path so operator snapshots stay consistent regardless of where pursuit runs.
  5. Step 5If state.json or staleness inputs are unreadable, regeneration fails closed and the conductor records the fault rather than publishing stale or empty dashboard rows.
