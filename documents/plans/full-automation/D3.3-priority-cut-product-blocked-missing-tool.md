# D3.3: priority cut product blocked missing tool

**Parent:** [D3-index](D3-index.md) · **Branch D** · **Vision §6** · **Release:** v2.16

## Reader narrative
<!-- prose-source: agent plane-d 2026-06-28 -->

Priority cut inverts normal scheduling when product pursuit is blocked on a missing playbook, script, or catalog entry: platform drain targeting that artifact class jumps ahead of routine K-step interleaving. The product goal stays blocked on H2 or internal wait until the promoted artifact exists or operator waives.

Cut prevents absurd ordering—five implement turns while the very playbook needed for implement sits queued at priority 50. Cut does not clear human gates; it only reprioritizes platform work that unblocks product. Enqueue triggers ([D2.1](D2.1-index.md)) may fire during the same blocked window; verify [D6](D6-index.md) checklist before marking promotion done.

## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cursor-agent 2026-06-28 -->

1. When product pursuit is blocked on a missing playbook, script, or catalog entry, preflight records the blocker and the scheduler applies priority cut—platform drain targeting that artifact class jumps ahead of routine [D3.1](D3.1-1-platform-turn-per-k-product-turns.md) K-step interleaving.
2. The product goal stays in blocked or internal-wait until the promoted artifact exists, [D6](D6-index.md) verify checklist passes, or an operator records an explicit H2 waiver—cut reprioritizes platform work but does not clear human gates.
3. Enqueue triggers from [D2.1](D2.1-index.md) may fire during the same blocked window; the conductor deduplicates promotion items for the missing class and elevates head priority so implement turns do not run while the needed artifact sits deep in the queue.
4. While cut is active, [D3.2](D3.2-priority-boost-queue-depth-threshold.md) depth boost is suspended so platform work that unblocks product precedes unrelated backlog-pressure drains.
5. If cut misidentifies the missing artifact, promotion completes without unblocking product, or queue state disagrees with journal blockers, pursuit halts at H2 until compose-first catalog resolution reconciles the blocker.
