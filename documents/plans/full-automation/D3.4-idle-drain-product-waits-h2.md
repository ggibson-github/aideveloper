# D3.4: idle drain product waits H2

**Parent:** [D3-index](D3-index.md) · **Branch D** · **Vision §6** · **Release:** v2.16

## Reader narrative
<!-- prose-source: agent plane-d 2026-06-28 -->

When product pursuit waits on external H2—credentials, legal approval, operator answer—CPU and conductor attention would otherwise idle. Idle drain spends that wait on platform queue processing: dequeue and promote without advancing product next_action or clearing gates.

Idle drain respects H2 semantics: no silent gate clearance, no fake implement progress. It maximizes parallel platform evolution during human latency and pairs naturally with autopilot loops that would otherwise spin on blocked checks. When H2 clears, product turns resume on prior next_action; platform backlog should be thinner than if waits were pure no-ops. See [A4.1](A4.1-stop-human-h1-h2-h3.md) stop taxonomy.

## Behavior / step logic
<!-- timeline-source: agent cursor-agent 2026-06-28 -->

1. When [A4.1](A4.1-stop-human-h1-h2-h3.md) classifies pursuit as blocked on external H2—credentials, legal approval, operator answer—check-pipeline-blocked holds product `next_action` while conductor attention would otherwise idle.
2. Idle drain routes autopilot or /loop turns to dequeue `state.platform.promotion_queue` items—playbook-keeper, script extraction, catalog rows—without advancing product `next_action` or clearing `gates_pending`.
3. Platform workers run under conductor spawn contracts with queue item ids; they may not dual-write consumer pursuit state, waive H2, or mark implement tasks complete during idle drain.
4. When the operator clears H2, pursuit resumes the frozen `next_action` and product lanes; journal records how many platform items drained during the wait.
5. If idle drain advances product scope, clears `blocking_questions`, or fakes implement progress while H2 remains open, validate-workflow fails closed and pursuit reverts to the prior blocked state.
